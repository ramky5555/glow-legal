Smiro Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 2026  ·  Last updated: March 2026

Smiro ("we", "our", or "the app") is a mindfulness application that uses smile detection to guide your meditation practice. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. We built Smiro with privacy as a core principle — your camera feed and facial data never leave your device.

The short version: Smiro processes your face on-device only. We never store, transmit, or share camera images or facial data. The only personal data we hold is your account credentials and session statistics.

1. Information We Collect

Account information

  • Your full name and email address, provided when you create an account.
  • These are stored securely in AWS Cognito, Amazon's managed authentication service.

Session statistics

  • Date and time of each session.
  • Session duration (minutes).
  • Smile seconds — the number of seconds you were smiling during the session.
  • Awareness score — a rolling percentage reflecting your smile activity over the session.

Session data is stored in AWS DynamoDB and is linked to your account. It is never shared with third parties.

Camera data

During an active session, Smiro accesses your device's front camera to detect whether you are smiling. This processing is performed entirely on your device using Google MLKit Face Detection (Android) and Apple Vision (iOS). No images, video frames, facial landmarks, or biometric identifiers are ever stored on your device or transmitted to any server. The camera is only active when a session is explicitly started by you.

Audio

Smiro plays ambient background music during sessions. The volume responds to your smile intensity in real time. No audio is recorded from your device's microphone.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To authenticate you and maintain your account.
  • To store and display your session history and progress.
  • To deliver the core in-session smile-detection experience.

We do not use your information for advertising, profiling, or any purpose beyond operating the app.

3. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third parties for marketing purposes. Data is processed by the following infrastructure providers solely to operate Smiro:

  • Amazon Web Services (AWS) — authentication (Cognito) and session data storage (DynamoDB).
  • Google Firebase (Firestore) — stores feedback messages you voluntarily submit through the app.
  • Google Sign-In — if you choose to sign in with Google, your Google account email and identity are shared with Google as part of the authentication flow.
  • Google MLKit — on-device face detection library (Android). No data is sent to Google.
  • Apple Vision — on-device face detection framework (iOS). No data is sent to Apple.

4. Data Retention

Your account and session data are retained for as long as your account is active. You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time from within the app (Profile → Delete Account). Deletion is immediate and irreversible.

5. Security

We use industry-standard measures to protect your data, including encrypted transmission (TLS), secure credential management via AWS Cognito, and access controls on our database. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we take these risks seriously and continuously review our practices.

6. Children's Privacy

Glow is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate data.
  • Request deletion of your data (available directly in the app).
  • Object to or restrict certain processing.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including the right to data portability and to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

If you are a California resident, you have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to delete it, and the right to opt out of its sale (we do not sell personal data).

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the address below.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of Smiro after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
